<ul>
	<li>Concept and Sketching</li>
</ul>
<p style="padding-left: 80px;">We will be using [overhead projectors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overhead_projector) as our performance area. The area is about 10 inches x 10 inches so generally, our puppets are small. They will be projected on a large screen, so that the audience sees these as 4 feet by 4 feet.</p>
<p style="padding-left: 80px;">We will work on cinematic concepts that use wide angles and closeups and transitioning between scenes.</p>
<p style="padding-left: 80px;">We will develop a story that is about 5-10 minutes long for each participant. The narrative can be loose and surreal or follow a literal narrative. You may come with your own ideas, or, if you're having trouble of thinking of ideas, we will look toward mythology and fairy tales to borrow from. An example is looking at the work of [Hans Christian Anderson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hans_Christian_Andersen). He even has a story about [Shadows](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WJBA1bidwis)!</p>
<p style="padding-left: 80px;">Your puppet short may have voiceover/narration, or music, or none if you prefer.</p>
<p style="padding-left: 80px;">If you have your idea, you can write your narrative, and storyboard. Here is an example of one that I did for a 3 Act performance in 2019.

It is not fancy, in fact it is pretty sketchy, but it lets me get my ideas from my head to paper to free my mind to work on cutting referencing my rough composition.</p>

	<li>[Lotte Reiniger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lotte_Reiniger)</li>
</ul>
<p style="padding-left: 80px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The best example to talk about animation using cutout paper silhouettes and shadows is Lotte Reiniger.</p>
<p style="padding-left: 80px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">This German artist was a pioneer of animation in the early and mid-20th century. Lotte used resources from the traditional shadow theater of the East and combined it with the openwork silhouettes of Europe.</p>
<p style="padding-left: 80px;">["Lotte Reinigers silhouettenfilm: Die Geschichte des Prinzen Achmed"](https://digitalcollections.oscars.org/digital/collection/p15759coll11/id/17107)</p>
<p style="padding-left: 80px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Booklet of the premiere of *<span style="font-weight: 400;">The Adventures of Prince Achmed*<span style="font-weight: 400;"> .</p>
<p style="padding-left: 80px;">[*The Star of Bethlehem*](https://archive.org/details/reiniger_the_star_of_bethlehem)</p>
<p style="padding-left: 80px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Beautiful short film from the 50's that uses silhouette animation to tell a popular Christmas story.</p>

<p style="padding-left: 80px;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Another magnificent short film from the 50's in which you will be able to appreciate the talent and originality of Lotte Reiniger.</p>
